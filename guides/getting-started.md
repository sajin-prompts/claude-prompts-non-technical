# Getting Started with Claude (No Technical Background Required)

This guide is for people who want to use Claude effectively — but have 
no interest in APIs, coding, or technical setup.

If you can open a browser and type, you can get value from Claude today.

---

## What Claude actually is

Claude is an AI assistant made by Anthropic. You talk to it like you 
would talk to a very knowledgeable colleague. It can read, write, analyze, 
summarize, explain, and help you think through problems.

You do not need to install anything. You do not need to code. You need 
a browser and a Claude account at claude.ai.

---

## The one mental shift that changes everything

Most people use Claude like a search engine — short question, expect 
short answer.

Claude works best when you treat it like a briefing with a smart colleague. 
The more context you give, the better the output.

**Instead of:** `Summarize this article`

**Try:** `I'm a middle school science teacher preparing a unit on climate 
change. Summarize this article focusing on anything relevant to 13-year-olds, 
and flag any terms I'd need to explain first.`

The second prompt takes 20 extra seconds to write. The output is ten 
times more useful.

This is the single biggest difference between people who find Claude 
underwhelming and people who find it transformative.

## Your first three sessions

**Session 1 — The Explainer**

Pick something you have always wanted to understand better. A concept 
from your field, a topic in the news, something a colleague keeps 
mentioning that you nod along to without really understanding.

Ask Claude to explain it to you at your level. Push back if it's unclear. 
Ask for examples. Spend 20 minutes just exploring.

Goal: get comfortable with the back-and-forth.

**Session 2 — The Real Work Task**

Take an actual task you need to do this week — an email you've been 
putting off, a report section, a decision you're avoiding. Bring it 
to Claude with full context. See what happens.

Goal: get your first real result from Claude.

**Session 3 — The Reusable Prompt**

Take the task from Session 2 and build a prompt you could use again 
next week for the same type of task. Save it somewhere. This is the 
beginning of your personal prompt library.

Goal: go from one-off use to a repeatable workflow.

## The three rules of getting good outputs

**Rule 1: Give context.**
Who you are, what you are trying to do, who the output is for. Claude 
does not know your situation unless you tell it.

**Rule 2: Specify format.**
Do you want a list? A paragraph? A table? An email? Tell Claude exactly 
what you need the output to look like.

**Rule 3: Iterate.**
The first response is a draft, not a final answer. Push back, refine, 
ask for changes. "Make this shorter", "make the tone warmer", "add 
an example" — Claude responds well to direct feedback.

---

## Common mistakes and how to avoid them

**Mistake:** Vague questions get vague answers.
**Fix:** Add "For context, I am [role] trying to [goal] for [audience]."

**Mistake:** Accepting the first response even when it's not quite right.
**Fix:** Say exactly what's off. "This is too long" or "Skip the intro 
and start with the main point."

**Mistake:** Treating Claude as a fact machine.
**Fix:** Claude can make mistakes on specific facts and recent events. 
For anything critical, verify with a primary source.

**Mistake:** Starting a new conversation for every question.
**Fix:** Keep related questions in one conversation. Claude remembers 
everything said earlier in the same session.
