# Work Productivity Prompts

These prompts help you handle the most common and time-consuming work 
tasks — faster and with better results.

No coding required. Copy, paste, and replace the parts in [brackets] 
with your own information.

---

## Prompt 1: The Email Writer

**What it does:** Turns your rough intention into a clear, professional 
email — saving you the time and stress of finding the right words.

**Best for:** Professionals, managers, anyone who writes a lot of emails.

**When to use:** You know what you need to say but not how to say it, 
or you're writing something sensitive and want to get the tone right.

**Copy this prompt:**
```
I need to write an email for the following situation:

Recipient: [WHO YOU ARE WRITING TO — their role and your relationship]
Purpose: [WHAT YOU NEED TO ACHIEVE — e.g., "follow up on an overdue 
payment", "decline a meeting request politely", "ask for a deadline extension"]
Key points to include: [LIST THE MAIN THINGS YOU NEED TO SAY]
Tone: [e.g., "professional and firm", "warm and apologetic", "direct but polite"]
Any context I should know: [ANYTHING RELEVANT — history, sensitivity, stakes]

Please write a complete email with subject line. Keep it concise and 
appropriate for a professional context.
```

**Tips:**
- The more context you give about the relationship, the better the tone
- Ask for 2 versions — one direct, one softer — and choose between them
- Always read it once before sending and adjust anything that doesn't 
  sound like you

## Prompt 2: The Meeting Summarizer

**What it does:** Turns messy meeting notes into a clean summary with 
decisions made, actions assigned, and next steps clear.

**Best for:** Managers, team leads, project coordinators, anyone who 
runs or attends a lot of meetings.

**When to use:** After any meeting where you need a record of what 
was discussed and decided.

**Copy this prompt:**
```
I'm going to paste my notes from a meeting. Please turn them into 
a clean meeting summary that includes:

1. Meeting purpose (1 sentence)
2. Key decisions made (bullet points)
3. Action items (who is doing what, by when — based on what's in the notes)
4. Open questions or issues that weren't resolved
5. Next steps and follow-up required

Format it so I can paste it directly into an email to send to attendees.

Here are my notes:
[PASTE YOUR RAW MEETING NOTES HERE]
```

**Tips:**
- Even messy, incomplete notes work — Claude fills in structure
- Add "keep it under 200 words" if you need a shorter summary
- Ask "draft the follow-up email using this summary" to save more time

## Prompt 3: The Project Planner

**What it does:** Takes a goal or project and breaks it into a realistic, 
sequenced action plan with timelines — so you know exactly what to do first.

**Best for:** Anyone managing a project, launching something new, or 
feeling overwhelmed by a complex goal.

**When to use:** You have a goal but don't know how to break it into 
manageable steps.

**Copy this prompt:**
```
I need to plan the following project:

Project goal: [WHAT YOU ARE TRYING TO ACHIEVE]
Deadline: [WHEN IT NEEDS TO BE DONE]
Resources available: [YOUR TIME PER WEEK, ANY BUDGET, TEAM OR SOLO]
Main constraints or challenges: [ANYTHING THAT COMPLICATES THIS]

Please create:
1. A breakdown of the main phases of this project
2. Specific tasks within each phase
3. A suggested sequence and rough timeline
4. The 3 most important things to get right for this to succeed
5. The biggest risks and how to mitigate them

Be realistic about what's achievable given my constraints.
```

**Tips:**
- Be honest about your actual time and resources — an unrealistic plan 
  is worse than no plan
- Ask "what should I do in the first 48 hours to build momentum"
- Revisit this with Claude weekly to adjust as things change
