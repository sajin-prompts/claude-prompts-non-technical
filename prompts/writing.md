# Writing & Editing Prompts

These prompts help you write better, faster — whether you're drafting 
something from scratch or improving what you already have.

No coding required. Copy, paste, and replace the parts in [brackets] 
with your own information.

---

## Prompt 1: The First Draft Generator

**What it does:** Takes your rough ideas and turns them into a structured 
first draft — so you're editing instead of staring at a blank page.

**Best for:** Writers, bloggers, professionals, students, anyone who 
struggles to start.

**When to use:** You know what you want to say but can't get it onto 
the page.

**Copy this prompt:**
```
I need to write [TYPE OF CONTENT — e.g., "a blog post", "an email", 
"a report section", "a cover letter"] about [TOPIC].

My audience is [WHO WILL READ IT — e.g., "my manager", "potential clients", 
"university admissions committee"].

The tone should be [TONE — e.g., "professional but warm", "casual and 
direct", "formal and authoritative"].

Here are my rough ideas and key points I want to cover:
[PASTE YOUR NOTES, BULLET POINTS, OR ROUGH IDEAS HERE]

Please write a first draft I can edit and improve.
```

**Tips:**
- The more specific your notes, the better the draft
- After the draft, ask: "Make the opening stronger" or "Cut this by 30%"
- If the tone is wrong, say exactly what's off: "Too formal" or "Too casual"

## Prompt 2: The Editor

**What it does:** Reviews your existing writing and gives you specific, 
actionable feedback — like having a skilled editor read your work.

**Best for:** Anyone who has written something and wants it to be better 
before sending or publishing.

**When to use:** You have a draft and want honest, useful feedback.

**Copy this prompt:**
```
Please review the following piece of writing. I need feedback on:

1. Clarity — are there sentences or sections that are confusing or unclear?
2. Structure — does the piece flow logically from beginning to end?
3. Tone — is the tone consistent and appropriate for [YOUR AUDIENCE]?
4. Strength — what are the weakest parts that need the most work?
5. Quick wins — what are 3 specific changes that would most improve this?

Please be direct and specific. I want honest feedback, not just praise.

Here is my writing:
[PASTE YOUR DRAFT HERE]
```

**Tips:**
- Tell Claude your audience so it can judge tone correctly
- Ask "Rewrite the weakest paragraph" after getting feedback
- Use this before sending any important email, report, or application

## Prompt 3: The Tone Adjuster

**What it does:** Takes something you've written and rewrites it in a 
different tone — more formal, more casual, more confident, more empathetic.

**Best for:** Professionals who write for different audiences, anyone 
who struggles to match tone to context.

**When to use:** Your writing is saying the right things but in the 
wrong way.

**Copy this prompt:**
```
Please rewrite the following text to be more [TARGET TONE — e.g., 
"professional and confident", "warm and approachable", "concise and direct", 
"formal and respectful"].

Keep all the key information and meaning intact. Only change the tone 
and style, not the substance.

Original text:
[PASTE YOUR TEXT HERE]

After rewriting, briefly explain the main changes you made and why.
```

**Tips:**
- You can combine tones: "more formal but still friendly"
- Ask for 2 versions with different tones to compare
- Works on anything — emails, reports, messages, social posts
